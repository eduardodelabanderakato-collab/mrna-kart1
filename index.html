LEVEL 1 — TRANSCRIPTION · NUCLEUS

You are RNA Polymerase, the enzyme that reads DNA and builds mRNA.
The Nucleus contains the DNA template.
Click the correct DNA Nucleotide (A, T, G, C) in sequence.
Each DNA base pairs with its complementary mRNA Nucleotide (A→U, T→A, G→C, C→G).
Adenine (A) in DNA becomes Uracil (U) in mRNA — Thymine is not used in RNA!
Complete all 8 nucleotides to finish the Messenger RNA (mRNA) strand.

LEVEL 2 — TRANSLATION · CYTOPLASM

You are the Ribosome, the molecular machine that reads mRNA.
The Cytoplasm is where protein assembly happens.
The mRNA is read in 3-letter chunks called Codons.
Click the correct tRNA — each carries an Anticodon that base-pairs with the Codon.
Each correct tRNA delivers an Amino Acid to the chain.
Complete all codons to build the Amino Acid Chain (Protein)!

mRNA STRAND COMPLETE!

The mRNA exits the Nucleus through the Nuclear Pore and enters the Cytoplasm.
The Ribosome will now read the codons and begin Translation.

Nuclear Pore — A channel in the nuclear membrane that allows mRNA to travel from the Nucleus to the Cytoplasm.

Codon — Every 3 nucleotides in an mRNA strand form a codon. Each codon codes for a specific amino acid.

Ribosome — The molecular machine in the Cytoplasm that reads mRNA codons and assembles the protein.
